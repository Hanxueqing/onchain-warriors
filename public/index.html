⚔ HOW TO PLAY

The Way of the Onchain Warrior

Built on the Azuki TCG tradition. Read once, master forever. Scan a wallet, draft a deck, battle in 60 seconds.

1 · Overview

Onchain Warriors is a 4-on-4 auto-battler. Your wallet becomes your hero. You draft 3 allies, set positions, then watch the battle play out deterministically over up to 6 rounds.

Battles are verifiable: a single seed determines every crit, every dodge, every roll. Two players with the same seed see the same result — no hidden black box.

⚡
A match takes about 60 seconds.
Read these rules once. After that, the battles play themselves.

2 · The 8 Warriors

Eight unique cards. Each wallet maps to one based on its onchain behavior. Full stats and signature skills below — all 8 use the same HP formula HP = 80 + DEF × 2.

ROUND_START Fires at the start of a round, before any attacks. ON_ATTACK Triggers when this unit is about to attack. REACTIVE Fires when this unit is being attacked. PASSIVE Always active — no energy cost.

3 · 2×2 Formation

Every team has 4 cards in a 2×2 grid. Position matters.

FL · Front LeftFR · Front Right
BL · Back LeftBR · Back Right
  • Front row takes hits first. Place your tanks here.
  • Back row is protected — only attacked after both front cards fall (or by pierce skills).
  • Same-column matchups are preferred when targeting (FL hits FL, FR hits FR).
  • Click two cards in your draft formation to swap their positions.

4 · Round Flow

Battles last up to 6 rounds. Each round flows in 3 phases:

  1. 1
    Round Start
    All alive units gain +1 energy (max 3). ROUND_START skills check conditions — Whale Devour, Chain Seal, Divine Insight may fire here.
  2. 2
    Action Order
    All 8 units (both teams) act in order of SPD, highest first. Ties broken by position (FL→FR→BL→BR), then by ID.
  3. 3
    Attack Resolution
    Each acting unit: select target → ON_ATTACK skill check → roll crit → calculate damage → BEFORE_DAMAGE defensive skill check → apply damage. Each unit may use its skill at most once per battle.

5 · Damage & Crit

HP Formula
maxHp = 80 + DEF × 2
Damage Formula
damage = ceil( max(5, ATK × elem × crit × skill − DEF × 0.25) )
  • Crit chance = min(LUCK, 30) — capped at 30% to prevent swing.
  • Crit multiplier = ×1.5 (down from ×2 — less random, more strategic).
  • Floor = 5 minimum damage — two tanks can't farm each other for 0 forever.
  • Berserk Slash disables crit — speedsters can't stack ATK + crit + element + burst all at once.

8 · Targeting Order

Every normal attack picks a target using this strict priority:

  1. Pierce skills override everything — Cross-Chain Strike and Preemptive Strike pick their own target.
  2. Aegis Guard taunt — Diamond Samurai in front row pulls all aggro (pierce bypasses).
  3. Same-column front — FL hits FL, FR hits FR if alive.
  4. Any front row — pick FL then FR.
  5. Same-column back — only if both fronts are KO'd.
  6. Any back row — pick BL then BR.

9 · Victory & Tiebreakers

Primary win: KO all 4 enemy cards.

If the 6-round cap is reached, the battle ends in a deterministic tiebreaker:

  1. More surviving units wins.
  2. If equal: higher total HP% wins.
  3. If equal: higher total damage dealt wins.
  4. If equal: higher team SPD sum wins.
  5. If still equal: deterministic seeded coin flip.

10 · Honor & Competitive

Two tracks, separate purposes:

🏅 HONOR
Only goes up. Counts lifetime wins. For showing off.
  • 0 → Initiate
  • 1–19 → Bronze Ronin
  • 20–99 → Silver Samurai
  • 100–299 → Golden Daimyo
  • 300–999 → Diamond Shogun
  • 1000+ → Onchain Emperor
⚔️ COMPETITIVE
Win +25, Loss −15. Reflects real skill. Wallet challenges don't count (anti-farm).
  • 0–799 → Bronze
  • 800–999 → Silver
  • 1000–1199 → Gold
  • 1200–1499 → Platinum
  • 1500–1999 → Diamond
  • 2000–2499 → Master
  • 2500+ → Grandmaster

11 · Game Modes

🤖 vs AI
Random AI deck. Counts for both Honor and Competitive. Best to learn the game.
🎯 Challenge Wallet
Scan any wallet → battle its onchain identity. Honor counts, Competitive does not (prevents farming weak wallets).
📡 Live PVP
WebSocket matchmaking. Both players consent. Counts for everything. Falls back to AI after 8s.

12 · Verifiable RNG

Every battle has a public seed. Given (decks, seed), the entire battle is reproducible — crits, dodges, AI choices, everything.

roll = FNV1a-hash(`seed | roundIndex | actionIndex | source | target | label`) % 100

After a battle, the seed is shown on the Victory screen and in the share-to-X text. Anyone can verify a match outcome.

→ Why this matters: in live PVP, both clients use the server-provided seed → both render the same battle without trusting each other.
